# Phishing Email Analysis Report

## 📧 Overview
This report analyzes a sample phishing email to identify common indicators of malicious intent. The goal is to understand how attackers use deception techniques to trick users into revealing sensitive information.

---

## 🧪 Sample Email Summary
- **Subject:** Urgent: Your Account Has Been Suspended  
- **Sender:** support@paypaI.com  
- **Claim:** Account suspension due to suspicious activity  
- **Action Requested:** Click a link to verify account  

---

## 🚩 Identified Phishing Indicators

### 1. Email Spoofing
- The sender email appears as **support@paypaI.com**
- The letter **“l” is replaced with uppercase “I”**
- This is a common spoofing trick to mimic a legitimate domain

---

### 2. Suspicious Link
- Provided link: http://paypal-security-check.verify-user.com/login
- The domain is not official
- Legitimate PayPal links always use: https://www.paypal.com/


---

### 3. Use of Urgent Language
- Phrases like:
- “Urgent”
- “Verify immediately”
- “Permanent suspension”
- These create panic and pressure users to act quickly without thinking

---

### 4. Lack of Personalization
- The email begins with **“Dear Customer”**
- Legitimate companies usually address users by their real name

---

### 5. Insecure Protocol (HTTP)
- The link uses **HTTP instead of HTTPS**
- Secure websites always use encryption (HTTPS)

---

### 6. Mismatched Domain
- The visible link suggests PayPal
- Actual domain is: verify-user.com
- This is a strong indicator of phishing

---

### 7. Social Engineering Techniques
The attacker uses:
- **Fear:** Threat of account suspension  
- **Urgency:** 24-hour deadline  
- **Authority:** Pretending to be PayPal  

---

## 🛠 Tools That Can Be Used
- Online email header analyzers  
- Email clients (for inspecting full headers)  
- URL checking tools  

---

## ✅ Recommended Actions
- Do not click suspicious links  
- Verify sender through official website  
- Report the email as phishing  
- Delete the email immediately  

---

## 🧠 Conclusion
This email is a phishing attempt. It uses multiple deceptive techniques such as spoofed sender addresses, fake URLs, urgency tactics, and social engineering to trick the recipient into revealing sensitive information.
